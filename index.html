Hello!

My name is Will Altman, and I am a second-year Computer Science and Engineering student at The Ohio State University.


I began my programming journy my Sophomore year of highschool when I took my first of 9 AP classes, AP Computer Science. Since then, programming has become one of my proudest passions and and something that I will never stop learning about. But I'm a real person too, not just some programming zombie. I've been playing the trumpet since 2010 and am fiercly competitive when it comes to auditioning for ensembles.

My proudest musical achievement was earning a spot in TBDBITL The Ohio State University Marching Band, also known as The Best Damn Band In The Land by beating out nearly 300 other musicians in a series of grueling three hour long endurance based marching "on-the-lines" in 100 degree Ohio summer heat. Since then I've performed infront of crowds ranging from a few hundred people to well over a few hundred thousand people, had a close up on national TV, and learned how to manage a full courseload while dedicating nearly 35 hours a week to a band.

Besides Music, I'm also a huge movie buff. I normally try (before covid) and go to the theater 2 or 3 times a month to see the best (and worst) new movies. I've also written dozens of short essays analyzing all different aspects of classic and award-winning films in film analysis classes throughout highschool and college. I also enjoy helping and teaching people programming. This semester I began working as a grader for a 2000-level software class here at OSU. I help run two closed lab sessions and host office hours every week to help students wrap their head around assignments. I am also responsible for grading homework and projects where I give strenuous feedback on code efficiency, programming style, and output accuracy. I am also taking an online class on Udemy where I am learning full-stack web development, and I am also a member of the Competitive Programming Club.
