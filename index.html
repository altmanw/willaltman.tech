Hello!

My name is Will Altman, and I am a second-year Computer Science and Engineering student at The Ohio State University.


I started programming as a Sophomore at Walnut Hills High School in Cincinnati, Ohio, when I took Advanced Placement (AP) Computer Science. This course was just one of the nine AP courses I took before college. Ever since, programming has become one of my deepest passions, and I will always endeavor to continue learning about and perfecting my skills in this area. Although coding is my passion, I have other interests and pursuits.

I’ve been playing trumpet since 2010 and am fiercely competitive when it comes to the craft. One of my proudest musical achievements was earning a spot, as an incoming freshmen, on The Ohio State University Marching Band (OSUMB), also famously known as TBDBITL. The Best Damn Band In The Land To make the marching band, I commuted three and a half hours, roundtrip, from Cincinnati to Columbus three times a week to practice with 500 other musicians who were all vying for their spot in the 228-strong ensemble. Nothing was guaranteed, however. Attending these practices only prepared you for tryouts and did not merit you a spot in the band. Tryouts themselves were grueling: four straight days of dawn to dusk competition in endurance-based marching exercises where multiple pairs of eyes were evaluating every aspect of your technique. Consistency and precision were paramount, and something as trivial as not pointing your toe enough during one session was enough to get anyone cut. After it was all said and done, my hardwork and dedication paid off and secured me a spot while 350 others went home empty handed. As a member of the OSUMB, I’ve performed in front of crowds exceeding 100,000 spectators and have been filmed up close on live, national TV broadcasts. During the football season, the band demands 30 hours a week on average for learning new routines, new music, and continuing to perfect the band’s trademark marching precision. All the while, I’ve successfully completed a full academic engineering course load, making the Dean’s list each semester.

Besides music, I’m also a huge movie buff. Before the pandemic, I would normally go to the theater two or three times during a month to see the best (and worst) new movies. I’ve also written dozens of short essays analyzing all sorts of different aspects of classic and award-winning films in the three film analysis classes I have taken throughout highschool and college.

I also enjoy teaching, and since August 2020, I’ve been a Teaching Assistant (TA) in the Software Components course at OSU. As a TA, I grade projects and homework on behalf of the professor. In addition, I help run two closed lab sessions and host several office hours every week to help students succeed in their coursework. I assist them in every way I can, from hosting one-on-one private tutor sessions where I walk them through an assignment to debugging the trickiest code the students are asked to write throughout the semester.
